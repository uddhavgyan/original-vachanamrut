### 132th Tarang - Panchala 6

---

In Vikram Samvat <mark1>1877</mark1>, on the last <mark1>ninth tithi</mark1> of <mark1>Falgun month</mark1> — this ambrosial speech of Hari was revealed **॥१॥**

In the fort of Panchal village, in the court of King Hemant (Jinabhai) — the Lord was seated on the auspicious platform, upon His beautifully spread couch **॥२॥**

Wearing white dhoti, white turban, white upper garment, and clad in warm coverings to dispel the cold — surrounded by devotees **॥३॥**

That omniscient Lord, with a cheerful face, Himself addressed the saints—  
Having duly considered in various ways, I declare that which I have firmly resolved **॥४॥**

<mark1>The incarnations of the Lord occur in thousands; among them, there is no other incarnation equal to Krishna, who is endowed with all powers</mark1> **॥५॥**

All the moods and aspects present in the individual forms of other incarnations are fully manifested here (in Krishna); therefore, He is called <mark1>“the Lord Himself”</mark1> **॥६॥**

At the time of His birth in Mathura city, <mark1>Lord Lakshmipati</mark1> revealed Himself in <mark1>four-armed form</mark1> to His parents **॥७॥**

In Gokul, He showed His universal form to Yashoda, and in the great battle of Mahabharata, He granted Arjuna the vision of the <mark1>Virat form</mark1> **॥८॥**

To Arjuna alone was specially granted the vision of the <mark1>Bhuma (cosmic) form</mark1>, and from His own mouth He described to him His <mark1>supremacy as Purushottama</mark1> **॥९॥**

Since I am beyond the perishable (kshara) and superior even to the imperishable (akshara), therefore I am celebrated in the world and in the Vedas as “Purushottama” **॥१०॥**

Because of being accompanied by Radha and others, He also revealed <mark1>His lordship over Goloka</mark1>, and through the words of the gopis addressed to the Yamuna, He manifested that secret form of His which had remained hidden till now **॥११॥**

To Akrura, on the Kalindi (Yamuna), He showed the <mark1>form reclining on Shesha</mark1>, and to Brahma, through the vision of multiple universes, He imparted knowledge of His imperishable nature as well **॥१२॥**

In this manner, He manifested within Himself His manifold forms situated in different places, along with their divine opulences and potencies **॥१३॥**

<mark1>Therefore, Krishna alone is the greatest among all incarnations — having firmly resolved this, all those who take refuge in Me should perform devotion to Him</mark1> **॥१४॥**

In this way, the mind that becomes steady and unwavering in Krishna through prior knowledge of His greatness is called “akutobhaya” (free from all fear) **॥१५॥**

<mark>Even if such a person should ever falter in his own vows, because he is completely protected by Krishna, his ultimate destination remains supreme</mark> **॥१६॥**

Thus <mark1>Sri Hari described the forms such as Lakshmipati and others manifested within Himself, along with the opulences situated therein</mark1>. Hearing this, the saints began to worship Him, recognizing Him as the cause of all causes; and other householders too, delighted by drinking the nectar of His words, became joyful **॥१७॥**

---

**Thus concludes the one hundred and thirty-second wave(Tarang) composed by Shatananda in Shri Hari Vakya Sudhasindhu ॥१३२॥६॥**
