### 7th Tarang - Gadhada Pratham 7

---

In the Vikramaditya era of the Shaka Samvat, in the year denoted by Rasa (6) + Ashva (7) + Vasu (8) + Bhumi (1) (that is, in <mark1>1876</mark1> Shaka Samvat), on the tenth day of the bright fortnight, this ambrosial discourse of Shri Hari took place. **॥1॥**

In that Durgapur (Gadhada), in the fort of the 'Uttam' king (Dadakhachar), Lord Shri Hari, seated amidst an assembly of ascetics and householders, dressed in white garments, spoke to His people. **॥2॥**

Wherever spiritual topics are mentioned in the scriptures, even though they are present there, they remain extremely difficult to comprehend even for the learned. **॥3॥**

That (spiritual discourse) should be understood in its true form; thereafter, by means of the characteristics of the jiva and so on, it should be thoroughly examined through <mark1>anvaya and vyatireka</mark1> and conclusively discriminated. **॥4॥**

From that (such discrimination), the confusion arising from the mixture (intermingling) of the characteristics of the jiva and so on never arises. Then Svayamprakashananda, who was seated ahead, asked (the Lord). **॥5॥**

O Swamin! <mark1>Jiva, Ish, Brahma, and Krishna</mark1> — what are their distinct characteristics in reality (in their essential nature), through anvaya (connection/concurrence) and vyatireka (difference)? **॥6॥**

You are omniscient, therefore it is proper for You to explain them; for the desire to know them exists in my heart. When thus questioned, that Lord of all (the Prabhu) began to expound them in accordance with the Agamas. **॥7॥**

The state of identification (tadatmya) with the three bodies — gross and so on (subtle, causal) — is said to be the anvaya of the jiva, on account of which it attains the world (transmigration). **॥8॥**

Because of being self-luminous in the nature of consciousness (chidrupa), and because of qualities such as indestructibility (achchhedyatva) and so on, the vyatireka of this (jiva) is described. That state (of such vyatireka) is called liberation. **॥9॥**

The identification (tadatmya) of Ishvara with the three bodies — Virat and so on (Sutratma, Avyakruta) — this is said to be the characteristic of His anvaya; and the agency in creation and so on is also included therein. **॥10॥**

Being established in Brahman-nature, being beyond Maya, and His eternally liberated essential form — all this is said to be the vyatireka characteristic of that (Ishvara). **॥11॥**

Being the controller of Purusha, Maya, the sun and so on, and being the support of countless universes — this is said to be the anvaya characteristic of Brahma. **॥12॥**

Though pervading everything, being unattached like space, and being subordinate to Krishna in the form of His divine abode — this is said to be the vyatireka of that (Brahma). **॥13॥**

Being present as the witness in the hearts of all — of the perishable and the imperishable — this is the anvaya characteristic of <mark1>Krishna, known as Purushottama</mark1>. **॥14॥**

Sporting in divine play (lila-vihara) with <mark1>Radha and others in the supreme abode (Aksharadham)</mark1>, and remaining independent even when time and so on are withdrawn — this is said to be the vyatireka of that (Purushottama). **॥15॥**

Thus, by contemplating in reality through anvaya and vyatireka, the characteristics of the jiva and so on are to be known by the wise. **॥16॥**

Jiva, Ish, Brahma, and Krishna — and even in Maya — this real distinction has existed from beginningless time; know this with firm conviction. **॥17॥**

In this manner, that ambrosia-like discourse of Lord Hari was received with reverence by the assembly of sages; and that foremost of sages, as well as the other people present in the assembly, upon hearing it, attained supreme bliss. **॥18॥**

---

**Thus ends the seventh 'Tarang' composed by Shatananda Muni in Shri Harivakyasudhasindhu. ॥7॥**
