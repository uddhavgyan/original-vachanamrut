### Commentary on 7th Tarang  - Gadhada Pratham 7

----

**शकेति ।**  
“Shak” (indication of the era) — the type of prefix of this kind has already been explained earlier. On the tenth day of the bright fortnight of the month named Margashirsha, this ambrosial speech of Shri Hari manifested. **॥1॥**

**तत्रेति ।**  
On that date, in the city of Durgapur, in the fort of the king named Uttam (Dadakhachar), within the assembly of ascetics and householders, in the midst of the gathering of sadhus and householders who had come from many countries, seated on the excellent throne, wearing white and pure garments, Lord Shri Hari Himself, out of His causeless compassion and without anyone asking, clearly gave instruction to all the people present in His assembly. **॥2॥**

**अध्यात्मेति ।**  
In the scriptures — especially in the Upanishads, Shrimad Bhagavat, and other sacred texts — wherever the discussion in the form of knowledge that imparts the understanding of the nature of the soul, Supreme Soul, and so on is stated, that is “adhyatma-varta” (spiritual discourse). However, that spiritual discourse, even in those scriptural contexts, is extremely difficult for even the learned to understand in its true form. **॥3॥**

**याथातथ्येनेति ।**  
For this very reason, <mark1>through anvaya and vyatireka</mark1> — <mark>that is, the pervasion of one’s own nature in others and remaining distinct from them while established in one’s own nature</mark> — one should thoroughly consider them separately in this manner, and through definite conclusions, by means of the characteristics of the jiva and so on, one should know that spiritual discourse in its true form. **॥4॥**

**जीवेति ।**  
By thus knowing the spiritual discourse, the confusion arising from the mutual mixture of the characteristics of the four — jiva and so on — such as “Is this the characteristic of the jiva or of Ishvara?” — never remains.  
Thereafter, having heard the words of Shri Hari, Swayamprakashananda Muni, who was seated in His presence, asked Him a question. **॥5॥**

**जीवेति (प्रश्न) ।**  
O Lord! <mark1>jiva (living being), Ishvara (Virat and so on), Brahma (Aksharbrahma), and Krishna (Parabrahma)</mark1> — please graciously explain the true characteristics of all these, as well as of Yourself, distinctly through anvaya and vyatireka. **॥6॥**

**वक्तुमिति ।**  
O Omniscient One! You are capable of speaking. There is a desire in my heart to know these characteristics. When thus questioned, Sarveshwar Shri Hari spoke to that muni in accordance with the scriptures. **॥7॥**

**स्थूलेति ।**  
O Muni! The continuous experience arising from the beginningless egoism born of ignorance — “I alone am these” — in the three bodies of gross, subtle, and causal, is the anvaya characteristic of the jiva. It is because of this that the jiva has been subject to the world of birth and death since beginningless time. **॥8॥**

**चिदिति ।**  
That which is of the nature of consciousness, self-luminous, distinct from the body-sense, and indestructible — that is the vyatireka characteristic of the jiva. When, by the power of exclusive devotion to God, it abides in a state distinct from the three bodies, that is called liberation. And the same is stated in Shrimad Bhagavat:
> ***मुक्तिर्हित्वान्यथा रूपं स्वरूपेण व्यवस्थिति:॥ (भा. २-१०-६)***\
*“Liberation means — abandoning other forms (non-self — body, senses, mind, etc.) and abiding in one’s own nature (the nature of the self).”*

Here the word “jiva” is used in the singular in the sense of the class. **॥9॥**

**विराडिति ।**  
Identification (sense of oneness) with the three bodies of Virat, Sutratma, and Avyakrita, as well as the agency in creating, sustaining, and dissolving the world — this is stated as the anvaya characteristic of the Ishvara named Vairaj. **॥10॥**

**अवस्थानमिति ।**  
Abiding in the state of Brahma-nature, remaining beyond Maya, and abiding in the feeling “I am eternally free” — this is stated as the vyatireka characteristic of Vairaj Ishvara. Here also the word “Ishvara” is used in the singular in the sense of the class, because many Ishvaras are described due to many universes. **॥11॥**

**पुस्वस्येति ।**  
That which is indicated by the word Purusha — the Mahapurusha (Mulapurusha) and other similar principal Purushas; that which is indicated by the word Maya — Mahamaya (Mulaprakriti) and other similar principal Mayas (Pradhan Prakritis); and Surya and so on (here other deities are also included) — being the inspirer and controller of all these, and being the support of countless universes — this is stated as the anvaya characteristic of Aksharbrahma. **॥12॥**

**सर्वत्रेति ।**  
Even while pervading everywhere in Mulaprakriti, Purusha (Mulapurusha), and their effects, and while existing in the form of ruler and support, Aksharbrahma is unattached like space — just as space, though present in all elements from air to earth, remains completely free from any taint or association with them. In the same way, though situated in Mulaprakriti, Purusha, and their effects, Aksharbrahma remains completely unattached to them. Moreover, this is <mark1>the attendant abode of Shri Krishna Paramatma</mark1> — that is, it abides <mark1>in the mood of servant and in the form of the abode</mark1> in His service. This itself is stated as the vyatireka characteristic of this Aksharbrahma. **॥13॥**

###### ***(Uddhav Gyan - Special Note)** :- 
- *This comparison is based directly on the sentence given above. <mark>Anvayata indicates the all-pervading-controller-support form (the formless uniform effulgence of the murti of Shri Krishna Bhagavan), whereas vyatirekata indicates Akshardhama (formless - as place/ground form) and servant mood (with form - infinite crores of liberated souls + Mulapurusha).</mark>*

**क्षरेति ।**  
“Kshara” meaning in the context of perishable entities — “of all”, that is, of all kshara and akshara entities; among them, of kshara entities — that is, of Pradhana (Prakriti), Purusha, and their world of effects and so on; and of akshara entities — that is, of Aksharbrahma (dhama) and the liberated ones, Mahapurushas, etc., situated therein — abiding equally in the hearts of all these, in both kinds of entities, as witness and as inner controller (indwelling) — this is stated as the anvaya characteristic (the characteristic of pervading and abiding in all) of that One named Purushottama, that is, the Supreme among Purushas, who is <mark1>Shri Krishna Paramatma</mark1>. (Here the word “stated” should be taken from the previous shloka.) **॥14॥**

**लीलेति ।**  
Even when the disappearance (withdrawal) of time-bound entities, the effect-form Mulaprakriti, and Purusha occurs, and even when becoming invisible by His own will, in the supreme abode — which is known by the name <mark1>Aksharbrahma (Akshardhama)</mark1> and which is His own original place. With Radha and so on — <mark1>sporting in divine pastimes with Radha, Lakshmi, and infinite crores of liberated souls who have attained the akshara state</mark1>, engaging in delightful pastimes — this is stated as the vyatireka characteristic of <mark1>Shri Krishna</mark1>. **॥15॥**

**अन्वयेति ।**  
In this way, by contemplating through anvaya and vyatireka, the characteristics of jiva and so on should be known in their true form by intelligent devotees of God. **॥16॥**

**जीवेति ।**  
<mark1>jiva (living beings that sustain life), Ishvara (Vairaj and so on), Brahma (Aksharbrahma), and Krishna (Shri Krishna Paramatma)</mark1> — of all these, as well as of <mark1>the two kinds of Maya/Prakriti</mark1> (effect-form Pradhan Prakriti and cause-form Mula Prakriti)* — this is what was stated earlier —<mark1>there exists a real, true, and beginningless-eternal distinction of natures that are mutually different</mark1>. Know this definitively in this way.  
Here the anvaya and vyatireka characteristics of jiva and so on have been stated. Although Maya is not explicitly mentioned in them, and Maya is not included in the enumeration of the ultimate distinctions of these (jiva, Ishvara, Brahma, Krishna), nevertheless, one should know the characteristics of Maya through anvaya and vyatireka from its effect state (effect condition) and cause state (cause condition). **॥17॥**

###### ***(Uddhav Gyan - Special Note)** :- Effect-form Pradhan Prakriti and cause-form Mula Prakriti: Main comparison of the two forms of Prakriti in Sankhya philosophy:

<div style="font-size: 0.9em;">

| Characteristic       | Cause-form Mula Prakriti                    | Effect-form Pradhan Prakriti                   |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| State                | Unmanifest (unrevealed, state of equilibrium) | Manifest (revealed, state of disequilibrium)  |
| Condition of Gunas   | Complete equilibrium of sattva-rajas-tamas  | Imbalance of gunas (predominance of one)      |
| Form                 | Cause (original cause, seed form)           | Effect (result, developed form)               |
| Change               | Unchangeable, eternal                       | Changeable, subject to modification           |
| Role in Creation     | Primordial cause (root of all elements)     | Direct form of all elements of creation       |
| Other Names          | Mula Prakriti, unmanifest, Pradhana (in original sense) | Pradhana (in effect form), modifications of Prakriti |

 </div>

**इतीति ।**  
Thus, having respectfully heard the ambrosial words of Sarveshwar Shri Hari, Swayamprakashananda Muni as well as all the Hari-bhaktas present in the assembly experienced immense joy. **॥18॥**



> *I worship that Shri Hari, the son of Bhakti-dharma, who truly explains to His devotees the characteristics of jiva and so on. **॥1॥***

---

**Thus ends the seventh Tarang of the commentary named ‘Setumala’ — Shri Hari Vakya Sudha Sindhu, composed by Raghuviracharya, the son of Shri Harikrishna Bhagavan and an exclusive devotee of Shri Hari, the propagator of exclusive devotion.** ॥7॥**
